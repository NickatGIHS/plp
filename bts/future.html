<p>My first idea was that I wanted to pursue my love of coding, so I went to <a href="https://www.adelaide.edu.au/degree-finder" target="_blank">Adelaide Uni's Degree Finder</a> (and <a href="https://study.unisa.edu.au/" target="_blank">UniSA</a>'s, but I ended up deciding on Adelaide Uni) and found <span class="info" title="Guaranteed Entry Score (ATAR): 80"><a href="https://www.adelaide.edu.au/degree-finder/bengh_behsofts1.html" target="_blank">this Software Engineering degree</a></span> that I liked the sound of.
<br>Because I am aiming to get into that degree is, in part, why I decided to <a href="#bonus">present my PLP as a HTML </a> rather than a Google Site. It is helping me practise my coding skills.
</p>
<p>From looking at the requirements for that degree, I worked backwards into Year 12 subjects, and found that I would have to do Year 12 Mathematical Methods and 12 Physics (both SACE).
<br>As I am doing Year 12 Maths Methods this year, I only needed to figure out <a href="https://www.flipsnack.com/Glenungaihs/glenunga-curriculum-guide-2020/full-view.html" target="_blank">my path</a> for Physics.
<br>To get into 12 Physics you need a B or better in 11 Physics 1 & 2, and to get into that you need a C or better in both 10 Pre-Senior Science (which is covered by <a href="https://gihs.sa.edu.au/specialist-programs/ignite/" target="blank">IGNITE</a>) and Specialist Physics (which I am doing next semester).
<br>That is why I chose those courses when I was selecting subjects back at the end of Year 9.
</p>
<p>I also recently took <a href="https://joboutlook.gov.au/CareerQuiz" target="_blank">this Career Quiz</a> to double-check that I have chosen the right pathway, and these were my results:</p>
